Since it is of critical importance that 
autonomous systems, whether software or hardware, 
that interact with human beings (and perhaps other sentient beings as well) 
behave in an ethical manner, we consider six possible approaches to effecting this.
We argue that the first five approaches are unsatisfactory and defend the last approach, 
the approach we have taken. It involves discovering 
ethically relevant features and
